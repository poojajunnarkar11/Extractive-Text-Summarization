Obama received national attention in 2004, with his unexpected March primary win, his well-received July Democratic National Convention keynote address, and his landslide November election to the Senate. After graduation, he became a civil rights attorney and professor, teaching constitutional law at the University of Chicago Law School from 1992 to 2004. In 2008, Obama was nominated for president, a year after his campaign began, and after a close primary campaign against Hillary Clinton.
